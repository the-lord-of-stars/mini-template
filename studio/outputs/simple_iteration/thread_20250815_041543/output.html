Data Insights Report

Topic: evolution of research on sensemaking

Key Insights

Insight 1
The dataset shows a progression in the focus of research on sensemaking from 2010 to 2012. In 2010, the emphasis was on using social media for journalistic inquiry, highlighting the role of computational journalism and social media in sensemaking. By 2012, the focus shifted towards semantic interaction and user interaction in visual analytics, indicating a growing interest in how users interact with data to make sense of it. This suggests an evolution from application-specific sensemaking (journalism) to more generalizable methods (semantic interaction) in visual analytics.
Insight 2
The 'AuthorKeywords' from 2010 include terms like 'Computational Journalism' and 'Social Media', which reflect a focus on using digital platforms for sensemaking in journalism. In contrast, the 2012 keywords such as 'User Interaction', 'visualization', and 'analytic reasoning' indicate a broader interest in the cognitive processes and tools that support sensemaking across different domains.
Insight 3
The 2011 entry bridges the gap between these two focuses by discussing observation-level interaction with statistical models, suggesting an intermediate step in the evolution towards more sophisticated user interaction techniques in visual analytics.
