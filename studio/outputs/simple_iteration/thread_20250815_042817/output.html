import pandas as pd
from collections import Counter
import matplotlib.pyplot as plt

# Load the dataset
df = pd.read_csv('outputs/simple_iteration/thread_20250815_042817/dataset_selected.csv')

# Extract years and keywords
df['Year'] = df['Year'].astype(int)
keywords_by_year = df.groupby('Year')['AuthorKeywords'].apply(lambda x: ' '.join(x.dropna()))

# Count keyword frequencies by year
keyword_trends = {}
for year, keywords in keywords_by_year.items():
    keyword_list = keywords.split(', ')
    keyword_trends[year] = Counter(keyword_list)

# Plot the top keywords over the years
plt.figure(figsize=(10, 6))
for year, counter in keyword_trends.items():
    top_keywords = counter.most_common(3)
    for keyword, count in top_keywords:
        plt.bar(year, count, label=keyword)

plt.title('Top Keywords in Sensemaking Research (1999-2007)')
plt.xlabel('Year')
plt.ylabel('Keyword Frequency')
plt.legend(title='Keywords')
plt.xticks(list(keyword_trends.keys()))
plt.show()

# Analyze abstracts for trends
abstracts_by_year = df.groupby('Year')['Abstract'].apply(lambda x: ' '.join(x.dropna()))

# Simple word frequency analysis in abstracts
abstract_trends = {}
for year, abstract in abstracts_by_year.items():
    words = abstract.split()
    abstract_trends[year] = Counter(words)

# Print some basic facts
print("### Begin of facts")
print(f"Years covered: {df['Year'].min()} to {df['Year'].max()}")
print("Top keywords by year:")
for year, counter in keyword_trends.items():
    print(f"{year}: {counter.most_common(3)}")
print("### End of facts")

Insight 1
The research on sensemaking has evolved from focusing on individual cognitive processes to incorporating social and collaborative aspects. In 1999, the emphasis was on 'visual sensemaking' using visualization spreadsheets to help users make sense of complex data, as seen in the paper by E.H. Chi and S.K. Card. The keywords from this year highlight 'Information visualization' and 'Sensemaking'.
Insight 2
By 2007, the focus had shifted towards collaborative visual analytics, recognizing that sensemaking is often a social process. This is evident in the work by Jeffrey Heer and Maneesh Agrawala, which discusses design considerations for supporting social interaction in visual analysis environments. The keywords from this year include 'visualization', 'analysis', and 'collaboration', indicating a broader scope that includes social dynamics in sensemaking.
